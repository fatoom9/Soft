Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

The TMDB 5000 Movie Dataset provides an extensive overview of around 10,000 movies sourced from The Movie Database (TMDB). It includes a wide range of movie details, such as user ratings, revenue, genres, and information on cast and crew. Notably, certain columns contain multiple entries, special characters, and inflation-adjusted financial data. Columns like cast and genres feature multiple values separated by a pipe (|) character, allowing for a list of actors or genres per movie. Some columns, such as cast, also include special characters that do not need further cleaning for this analysis. Additionally, the budget_adj and revenue_adj columns provide budget and revenue figures adjusted to 2010 dollars, enabling standardized financial comparisons across different periods by accounting for inflation.

Key Columns¶

• id: A unique identifier for each movie entry.

• imdb_id: The unique ID of the movie on IMDb, useful for cross-referencing with IMDb’s extensive movie database.

• popularity: A score indicating the relative popularity of the movie.

• budget: The production budget for the movie in USD.

• revenue: Total revenue generated by the movie in USD.

• original_title: The official title of the movie at release.

• cast: A list of primary cast members, separated by |.

• homepage: The official website of the movie.

• director: Name of the movie’s director.

• tagline: A brief, memorable slogan or tagline associated with the movie.

• overview: A summary providing a short description of the movie plot.

• runtime: The movie’s duration in minutes.

• genres: A list of genres applicable to the movie, separated by |.

• production_companies: Companies involved in producing the movie, separated by |.

• release_date: The date the movie was released, formatted as MM/DD/YY.

• vote_count: The total number of votes the movie received on rating platforms.

• vote_average: The average rating given to the movie, typically on a scale of 1 to 10.

• release_year: The year in which the movie was released.

• budget_adj: The movie’s budget adjusted for inflation, providing a more accurate comparison over time.

• revenue_adj: The movie’s revenue adjusted for inflation, helping gauge financial success relative to other periods.

Limitations

Although this analysis provides valuable insights, there are several limitations to consider:

  1. Missing Data: Some columns contain missing values, which may slightly affect the accuracy of the results.

  2. Outdated Financial Adjustments: The budgets and revenues are adjusted only for inflation up to 2010, meaning that the financial data may not accurately reflect more recent economic conditions.

  3. Multiple Genres: Many movies are associated with more than one genre, which can complicate the analysis of trends specific to a single genre.

  4. Undefined Popularity Metric: The dataset does not provide a clear explanation of how popularity scores are calculated, limiting our ability to fully understand this metric.

  5. Data Limited to 2015: The dataset includes movies released only up to 2015, meaning it may not capture more current trends or patterns in the film industry.

  6. Lack of External Factors: The dataset does not account for external factors such as marketing efforts, global events, or cultural shifts, which can all influence a movie's success.

Conclusions¶

**Types: Popular films frequently fall into action, adventure, and science fiction genres, suggesting that these genres resonate widely with audiences, potentially due to their visual appeal and universal themes.

***Budget and Revenue: Higher budgets often align with increased popularity, likely due to larger marketing resources. However, revenue does not directly correlate with budget, indicating that factors beyond spending—like storyline, timing, and audience appeal—may play significant roles.
